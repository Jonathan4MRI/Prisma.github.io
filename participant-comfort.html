Essential Comfort Tips

Remember: The more comfortable a participant is, the better the scan quality will be. A relaxed participant moves less, resulting in clearer images and fewer sequence repetitions.

Before Positioning

Always ask about any existing back pain, neck pain, or joint discomfort the participant may have. Take extra time to add additional padding and support where needed. Make sure the participant feels heard and understood ‚Äì this initial investment in comfort pays dividends throughout the scan.

Temperature Management

The scanner room is kept cold for equipment optimal performance. Always offer participants a blanket, even if they initially decline. Consider using a weighted blanket for participants who are particularly cold or anxious ‚Äì the gentle pressure can be calming and help reduce movement.

Claustrophobia Support

If a participant expresses concerns about claustrophobia, ask Jonathan to join for the scan. He has extensive experience with various tips and tricks to help participants feel comfortable in the scanner environment. His presence can make the difference between a successful scan and a cancelled appointment.

During Scanning

Establish communication early by checking in with the participant after the first localizer scan. This reassures them that you can hear them and they can hear you. Continue to check in frequently between sequences, especially during longer scans. Provide time updates and encouragement throughout.

After the Scan

Slowly bring the participant out of the bore to prevent dizziness. Crucially, ensure the participant remains lying down until the bed is at its lowest position ‚Äì this prevents falls and injuries. Only then should you help them sit up gradually before standing.

Suite Maintenance

After each participant, thoroughly clean and disinfect all contact surfaces including the bed, coils, headphones, and squeeze ball. This maintains hygiene standards and ensures the suite is ready for the next participant.
