Ensuring participant comfort and safety during MRI scanning sessions

Positioning & Setup

[Photo: Proper head positioning in coil]

Head Positioning

Ensure the participant's head is centered in the coil with adequate padding around the sides. The top of the head should be aligned with the top of the coil.

Tip: Use foam pads to fill gaps and minimize head movement
[Photo: Knee support cushion placement]

Knee Support

Place a bolster under the knees to reduce lower back strain. This is especially important for longer scans.

Tip: Ask about back problems before positioning
[Photo: Proper blanket placement]

Temperature Comfort

Offer a blanket as the scanner room is kept cool. Ensure the blanket doesn't interfere with coil positioning.

Tip: Tuck blanket edges to prevent sliding during scan

Communication & Reassurance

[Photo: Emergency squeeze ball in hand]

Emergency Squeeze Ball

Place the squeeze ball firmly in the participant's hand. Demonstrate how to use it and assure them you'll respond immediately.

[Photo: Mirror setup showing outside view]

Mirror Positioning

Adjust the mirror so participants can see out of the bore. This helps reduce claustrophobia and provides visual connection.

[Photo: Headphone placement]

Audio Setup

Ensure headphones are comfortable and secure. Test audio levels before starting. Explain you can hear them between scans.

Special Considerations

Claustrophobic Participants

Keep feet out of bore when possible
Use fan for air circulation
Offer eye mask if preferred over keeping eyes open
Practice breathing exercises before entering
Consider feet-first positioning when protocol allows

Pediatric Participants

Allow parent/guardian to stay in room if possible
Show mock scanner or photos beforehand
Use child-friendly language to explain sounds
Offer to play calming music or stories
Use extra padding for smaller body sizes

Elderly Participants

Extra time for positioning and transfers
Additional padding for joint comfort
Clear communication about scan duration
Frequent comfort checks between sequences
Ensure hearing aids are removed and participant can still hear instructions

Quick Comfort Tips

Before Scanning: Have participant use restroom, remove all metal, and do a final comfort check
During Setup: Explain each step, work efficiently but don't rush, maintain calm demeanor
During Scanning: Check in between sequences, provide time updates, stay encouraging
After Scanning: Help participant sit up slowly, ensure they're steady before standing

Troubleshooting Comfort Issues

Motion from Discomfort

If participant is moving due to discomfort, pause and readjust. Better to take time repositioning than repeat sequences.

Anxiety During Scan

Talk between sequences, provide remaining time, offer short break if needed. Consider rescheduling if anxiety is severe.

Temperature Issues

Adjust fan speed, add/remove blanket between sequences. Be aware that anxiety can cause feeling hot.
