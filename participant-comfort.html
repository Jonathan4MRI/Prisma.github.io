Essential Comfort Tips

Note: Comfortable participants move less. Less movement means better scan quality.

Before Positioning

Ask about back pain, neck pain, or joint problems. Add extra padding where needed. Take time to ensure the participant is comfortable before starting.

Temperature Management

The scanner room is cold. Offer a blanket to all participants. Use a weighted blanket for participants who are cold or anxious.

Claustrophobia Support

If a participant mentions claustrophobia, ask Jonathan to assist with the scan. He knows techniques to help participants stay comfortable.

During Scanning

Check in after the first localizer to establish communication. Continue checking between sequences. Give time updates during longer scans.

After the Scan

Move the participant out of the bore slowly. Keep them lying down until the bed reaches its lowest position. Then help them sit up gradually before standing.

Suite Maintenance

Clean and disinfect the bed, coils, headphones, and squeeze ball after each participant.
